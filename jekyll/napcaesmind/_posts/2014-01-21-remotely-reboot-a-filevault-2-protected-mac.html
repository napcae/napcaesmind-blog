--- layout: post title: Remotely reboot a FileVault 2 protected Mac ---

So this is one is pretty neat. If your Mac is far away(or you are lying in your bed) and you want to restart your Mac, you can just sudo shutdown -r now.

But what if your Mac is FileVault 2 protected and encrypted? Who is typing the password for you into the keyboard? The answer? Just use sudo fdesetup authrestart and you are good to go!
